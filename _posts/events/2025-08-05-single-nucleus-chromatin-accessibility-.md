# Single-nucleus chromatin accessibility profiling identifies cell types and functional variants contributing to major depression
_Published on Tue, 05 Aug 2025 00:00:00 GMT_

Abstract
--------

Genetic variants associated with major depressive disorder (MDD) are enriched in the regulatory genome. Here, we investigate gene-regulatory mechanisms underlying MDD compared to neurotypical controls by combining single-cell chromatin accessibility with gene expression in over 200,000 cells from the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex of 84 individuals. MDD-associated alterations in chromatin accessibility were prominent in deep-layer excitatory neurons characterized by transcription factor (TF) motif accessibility and binding of NR4A2, an activity-dependent TF reactive to stress. The same neurons were enriched for MDD-associated genetic variants, disrupting TF binding sites linked to genes that likely affect synaptic communication. Furthermore, a gray matter microglia cluster exhibited decreased accessibility in individuals with MDD at binding sites bound by TFs known to regulate immune homeostasis. Finally, we identified gene-regulatory effects of MDD-risk variants using sequence-based accessibility predictions, donor-specific genotypes and cell-based assays. These findings shed light on the cell types and regulatory mechanisms through which genetic variation may increase the risk of MDD.

### [Extended Data Fig. 1 snATAC-seq study overview.](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41588-025-02249-4/figures/8)

Schematic of the snATAC-seq study design in 84 subjects with MDD (n = 44) and neurotypical controls (n = 40). Nuclei were combined from a male and female pair (either MDD or control) and pooled libraries were demultiplexed using chromatin accessibility at common variants (1000 Genomes[17](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41588-025-02249-4#ref-CR17 "The 1000 Genomes Project Consortium. A global reference for human genetic variation. Nature 526, 68–74 (2015).")) and validated using donor-specific genotypes obtained from the genotyping arrays. Cell-types were identified by unsupervised clustering of high-quality cells (>200,000) and integrated with snRNA-seq data from the same donors to identify genes regulated by MDD-associated differential chromatin regions, transcription factors (TFs), and genetic variants. Finally, gapped-kmer support vector machines (gkm-SVM) were used to predict cluster- and allele-specific accessibility alterations caused by MDD-associated genetic variants. Allele-specific gene-regulatory activity of MDD-risk variants were confirmed using donor-specific genotypes and in vitro reporter assays using cells that endogenously expressed TFs with disrupted binding sites. (Created in BioRender. Chawla, A. (2025) [https://BioRender.com/s86m244](https://biorender.com/s86m244)).
