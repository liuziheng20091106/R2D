# Comprehensive transcription factor perturbations recapitulate fibroblast transcriptional states
_Published on Wed, 06 Aug 2025 00:00:00 GMT_

Abstract
--------

Cell atlas projects have revealed that common cell types often comprise distinct, recurrent transcriptional states, but the function and regulation of these states remain poorly understood. Here, we show that systematic activation of transcription factors can recreate such states in vitro, providing tractable models for mechanistic and functional studies. Using a scalable CRISPR activation (CRISPRa) Perturb-seq platform, we activated 1,836 transcription factors in two cell types. CRISPRa induced gene expression within physiological ranges, with chromatin features predicting responsiveness. Comparisons with atlas datasets showed that transcription factor perturbations recapitulated key fibroblast states and identified their regulators, including _KLF2_ and _KLF4_ for a universal state present in many tissues, and _PLAGL1_ for a disease-associated inflammatory state. Inducing the universal state suppressed the inflammatory state, suggesting therapeutic potential. These findings position CRISPRa as a nuanced tool for perturbing differentiated cells and establish a general strategy for studying clinically relevant transcriptional states ex vivo.

### [Extended Data Fig. 2 Testing performance of droplet overloading model, related to Fig.](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41588-025-02284-1/figures/9) [1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41588-025-02284-1#Fig1).

**a**. To benchmark reconstruction accuracy, we split datasets into disjoint subsets, infer regression coefficients using a linear model, and assess reconstruction with various metrics. The simplest metric computes Pearson correlation between the (stacked) regression coefficient matrices. **b**. Visual comparison of regression coefficients inferred from singlets (left) vs. an equivalent number of doublets (right), either with (top) or without (bottom) stable gene-based normalization. Stable gene normalization removes differences caused by transcript capture efficiency, producing visually indistinguishable results.
